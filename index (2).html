The event featured 105 research papers across 34 sessions, covering topics like business management, accounting, finance, marketing, and digital transformation. Discussions focused on AI, fintech, investment trends, and the future of digital economies. For the second time, the student volunteer team successfully organized and managed the forum under the supervision of Dr. Hazar Hammoud, ensuring smooth operations and a professional experience for all attendees. The forum concluded with key recommendations emphasizing digital transformation, AI ethics, fintech development, and fostering research and innovation.
